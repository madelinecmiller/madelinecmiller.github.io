ABOUT


Hi! My name is Madeline Miller.
Durham, North Carolina

I study UX Design and Information Science at UNC SILS.

I love the creative logic that goes into the display and formatting of information. I believe that presentation determines how comprehensible any idea or task may be.

As an undergraduate at Reed College and UNC-CH, I studied the transatlantic slave trade's effect on the Americas. I developed my interest in storytelling by working on a documentary show for WXYC 89.3 fm. I find wonderment in the emotional pallette of music and the transitions between songs, genres, and tones.

After college, I continued a family tradition of working in the restaurant industry. I also taught Early Childhood education in a Montessori school, which I liken to being a UX designer.

Another part of my life and job is that I am a caregiver to children and elderly.

Currently, I'm on a path to having full-stack development capabilities and earning a master's degree in UX design.

I'm interested in making personal data a greater part of people's every day information use in positive ways, especially for the purposes of wellness and health;

music streaming, and the classification and utilization of [huge] music libraries such that the user can be introduced to material they love but that they likely would never have found;

information philosophy relating to truth on the web,"fake news," consent and privacy, bias in A.I., uplifting outliers and edge cases.
