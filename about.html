ABOUT

I'm a student of web design, development, user experience, and information science at UNC-CH.

I'm drawn to the creative logic that goes into the display and formatting of information. I firmly believe that presentation largely accounts for how comprehensible any idea may be and that this principle can be utilized to radically improve both education and our everyday information use.

In college, I studied the history of the transatlantic slave trade and practiced the delivery of information to an audience auditorily by making radio stories for UNC-CH's student radio station. After graduating in 2014, I taught early childhood education in a Montessori School. There, I directed my attention to presenting and formatting information so that it could instruct and capture the attention of young children.

Nowadays, I'm preparing for a master's degree at UNC-CH's School of Library and Information Science, with a focus on Human-Computer Interaction. In addition to my studies, I make a living caring for children and working in the restaurant industry. You can find my resume here.
